Enemies, but intelligent

Turki Abdulla Al sadiri

    Since a period which is not far; I wrote an article in which I warned from the future strategies of Iran and clarified that If Israel’s danger of extension or controlling some Arab areas close to it, although the Palestinians will keep owning the title of “the most affected victim;” G. C. C. countries have never been subjected to Israeli attempts of intervention, whether to their affairs or their borders. I clarified in that article that Iran is the first specialized hostile party against the Arabian Gulf group of countries. But such Iranian intervention is not similar to the Arab way of raising the slogan of “Hurray Arabs; return your glories” and then moving towards categories war. It is never like this. Iran is implementing many strategies for reaching an access into Arabian Gulf countries internal conditions.

The reaction of certain group of writers who are considering that your past enemy is your future enemy, too, was funny due to that such belief is not true. The most dangerous aggression is what is revealed from the policies of the Super Powers which superiority sometimes is strange as much as its violence for reaching certain targets at other times is strange, too. Accordingly; it is impossible to confirm for sure that certain allies are going to last continuously as they are the interests which are behind such allies. Iran is not a sectarian temple which is reacting according to its own stimuli all the times due to that this is just the external cover. The truth is that Tehran is still suffering the complex of loss or absence of the Super power which used to be 2nd international one thousand & four hundred years ago. They are still thinking how they can restore from the Bedouin Arabs what has become considered parts from their homelands. In such calculation; there are economic interests that constitute the first motive behind influence extension movements or seizing opportunities gain.

Look at Israel, too. The country of the small size and area that was looking at the vast Arab areas around it before 1967 looking for peace and security and how it occupied additional areas, tore the Arab presence in them, turned its back to peace agreements and started growing politically and militarily. It believed in a permanent peace imposed by its own power. It is, like Iran, standing behind each window and overlooking the nature of worrying Arab conditions near to which the starting points of new Arab conflicts rise.

When is the Arab World is going to rise itself up. It is not going ever to defeat the forces which are following up its bad conditions unless it gathers its categories, unify its conscious leaderships and unify its various directions instead of the exchangeable insults through the media and the promotion of wrong concepts of multiple perspectives.

There are no rapprochement characteristics at all between Iran and Israel but the motives that move them are interests’ strategies in the Arab World that constitute a danger for it.
